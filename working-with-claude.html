Willis v. Rendon Legal — The Case

The Situation

An opposing attorney filed a false Certificate of Last Known Address — using a decade-old address — just 103 days after personally serving me at my actual location. A default judgment stripped me of custody for 490+ days without my knowledge.

The Response

Filed pro se in Harris County, 189th Judicial District. Second Amended Petition, Response to Motion to Dismiss, Bill of Review — all co-authored with Claude. Active criminal investigation (Precinct 4 Constable, Incident #2601-01381). State Bar and TREC complaints filed.

Trial set: February 2, 2027  ·  189th District Court  ·  Judge Tamika Craft-Demming

How Claude Helped Build the Case

Petition DraftingCo-authored the Second Amended Petition — 4 causes of action, statutory citations, structured prayer for relief
Motion ResponseDefeated attorney immunity + judicial privilege arguments by identifying the 103-day timeline as dispositive
Legal ResearchCantey Hanger, Landry's, Troice v. Greenberg Traurig — surfaced and contextualized key Texas precedent
Strategic FramingPositioned the false certificate as conduct "foreign to attorney duties" — the exact language that defeats immunity
